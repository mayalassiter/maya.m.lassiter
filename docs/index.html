Current Work

I am pursuing my MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University. I am a member of the Chamanzar Group working on polymer neural probes for optogenetics applications. I am also a teaching assistant for the undergraduate course: Electronic Devices and Analog Circuits.

Optogenetics

I am working on developing a fabrication process for biocompatible, flexible, polymer waveguides. Our work focuses on co-locating nerural recording and optical stimulation volumes in the brain. A recent poster can be seen here.

We hope these probes can be used for neuroscience research. The early stages of my research has been to develop light source bonding processes such as using a single optical fiber and UV curable epoxy as seen below.

Piezoelectric Resonators

Over the 2017 academic year, the MEMS fabrication course sequence has introduced the theory, design, analysis, fabrication, and characterization of Aluminum Nitride resonators. The project has included using the Carnegie Mellon Nanofabrication facility.
