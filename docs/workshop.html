I taught a workshop on making electronics responsibly.

With the help of Prototype PGH I taught a workshop on the history of nanofabrication with a particular focus on the multi-ethnic majority female workforce who faced a simultaneous lack of safety in the workplace and dearth of information about the chemical processes they were expected to carryout.

Here is a resource list that I used to prepare the workshop:

Workshop References

  1. The World in a Grain by Vince Beiser
  2. The Silicon Valley of Dreams: Environmental injustice, Immigrant Workers, and the High-Tech Global Economy by David Naguib Pellow and Lisa Sun-Hee Park
  3. Behemoth: A History of the Factory and the Making of the Modern World by Joshua B. Freeman
  4. The Cobalt Pipeline in The Washington Post (2016) by Todd C. Frankel
